💡 What is Digital Literacy?

Digital literacy is the ability to use devices, apps, websites, and online platforms effectively, responsibly, and safely for communication, learning, and problem-solving.

🛠 Useful Digital Tools

Google DocsAssignments, notes, teamwork
GitHubProjects, coding, portfolio
CanvaPosters, slides, visual design
LinkedInNetworking and career growth
Google FormsQuizzes, surveys, feedback
DriveCloud storage and sharing

🔐 Safe Internet Practices

Use strong passwords
Enable 2-factor authentication
Never share OTP or PIN
Avoid suspicious links
Verify before sharing
Download only from trusted sources

💼 Professional Online Presence

  • Keep your profiles updated
  • Use clear and formal language
  • Show your skills and projects
  • Share useful and positive content
  • Build a trustworthy online image

📧 Email Etiquette

  • Write a clear subject line
  • Use a respectful greeting
  • Keep your message short and structured
  • Check grammar and spelling
  • End with a proper sign-off

🚀 Why It Matters

Digital literacy helps students study better, communicate clearly, avoid online scams, and prepare for future internships, careers, and responsible digital citizenship.
